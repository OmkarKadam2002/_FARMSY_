Nitrogen (N) :


This field represents the amount of Nitrogen (in kg/ha) present in the soil for the crop. Nitrogen is an essential nutrient required for plant growth, and its deficiency or excess can affect the growth as well as the yield of the crop.

Phosphorus (P) :


This field represents the amount of Phosphorus (in kg/ha) present in the soil for the crop. Phosphorus is another essential nutrient required for plant growth and is important for processes such as energy transfer and photosynthesis.

Potassium (K) :


This field represents the amount of Potassium (in kg/ha) present in the soil for the crop. Potassium is also an essential nutrient required for plant growth and plays a role in various physiological processes such as water regulation and nutrient transport.

Soil pH :


This field represents the soil pH during the crop's growing period. pH is a measure of the acidity or alkalinity of the soil and can affect the availability of nutrients for the crop.
